# Checklist — Adding a New Weapon

Turnkey numbered checklist for adding a boss-reward weapon (or the starting buster) end to end.

1. **Create the `WeaponData` resource** (`.tres`) — id, display name, icon reference, archetype tag (matches one of the 8 weakness-cycle archetypes: Frost/Flame/Storm/Volt/Stone/Toxin/Blade/Gravity), base damage, ammo cost per shot, and the `weakness_target` field (below).
2. **Build the projectile scene and register it with the projectile pool.** Every weapon's projectile must be pool-backed, not instantiated/freed per shot — see `gotchas.md` #10 for why unpooled projectiles silently fail mid-boss under load. Size the pool to the worst realistic case (see step 8 of that gotcha, and cross-check against this weapon's fire rate x max charge-tier count if it has charge behavior).
3. **Wire the HUD meter.** Every non-buster weapon has a visible ammo meter (distinct from the player's HP bar) that depletes on fire and (if the design allows) regenerates or refills at pickups. Confirm the meter updates in the same frame the ammo value changes — a delayed/desynced meter reads as a bug even when the underlying ammo count is correct.
4. **Define charge-tier behavior**, if this weapon supports charging. Default assumption is it follows the same tap/0.55s/1.1s charge-tier timing as the buster (see `references/design/game-feel.md`) unless the weapon is deliberately designed as a non-chargeable single-tier weapon. If this weapon is **arm-upgrade gated** (i.e., its charge tiers only unlock after a separate arm-upgrade item, a common genre pattern), explicitly gate the higher tiers behind that WorldFlag rather than assuming charge access is universal from the moment the weapon is granted.
5. **Define mobility interactions**, if any. Some weapons in this genre double as traversal tools (a weapon that lets the player create a temporary platform, freeze a hazard into a safe surface, hook/pull to a surface, etc.). If this weapon has such an interaction, document exactly which stage gimmicks or previously-blocked paths it unlocks, and add the corresponding WorldFlag-gated shortcut to the relevant stage's checklist (see `checklists/new-stage.md` step 9) — a mobility weapon with no unlocked content anywhere is a wasted design slot.
6. **Assign `weakness_target` and check cycle integrity.** Set which boss archetype this weapon is strong against. Then re-derive the **full 8-node cycle** and confirm:
   - Every boss archetype is the weakness target of **exactly one** weapon (no boss with zero counters, no boss double-countered while another has none).
   - **No self-loop**: a boss must never be weak to the weapon it itself grants as a reward (that would make the reward useless against its own source and breaks the traversal logic of "beat boss A's weakness, get weapon, use it on boss B").
   - The assignments **form one single cycle** (A beats B beats C beats ... beats A) rather than splitting into two or more disconnected sub-cycles or a chain with a dead end. A dead-end chain (a boss with no weapon that beats it, or a weapon with no boss it beats) breaks the "always a viable next target" property that lets players choose their own stage order. See `gotchas.md` #9 for the load-time validation that should catch this automatically going forward.
7. **Define ammo economy.** Set max ammo capacity, per-shot cost (potentially higher for higher charge tiers), and where refills come from (pickups, sub-tank equivalent, full refill on continue/checkpoint if that's the genre convention chosen for this project). Confirm the economy allows a full weakness-kill (per `checklists/new-boss.md` gate 10) without running out of ammo under reasonable play — an ammo-starved weakness weapon defeats the purpose of the reward loop.
8. **Author SFX and icon.** Distinct fire/charge SFX per `assets/audio-spec.md` SFX list conventions, and a HUD icon distinguishable at a glance from every other weapon's icon (shape and color both, not color alone, for accessibility).
9. **Playtest gate.** Confirm this weapon is usable across all locomotion states via the shoot-overlay convention (see `assets/spritesheet-spec.md`), does not desync the HUD meter, and produces the expected weakness-kill time against its target boss (cross-reference `checklists/new-boss.md` gate 10).
